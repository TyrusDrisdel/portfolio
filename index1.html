MIT

MIT was founded by William Barton Rogers on April 10, 1861. MIT is located in Cambridge, Massachusetts. It grew into one of the best universities for computer science and engineering. The campus stretches 168 acres across an urban setting. MIT was created to accelerate the nations's industrial revolution. MIT's main focus is focused on technology and engineering. MIT's mission is to progress knowledge and educate people in technology, science, and other ares of prestige that will serve the nation in great ways. Some graduates of MIT turn out to be extremely successful, such as astronauts, CEOs of companies, actors, and more.

Requirments & Cost

To have a great chance to get into MIT, you should be near the 75th percentile, and have a 1570 SAT score or a 36 ACT. Your GPA should be at or over a 4.17 GPA. If your GPA is lower than 4.17, your SAT/ACT will have to make up for your GPA. MIT has an exceptance rate of 7%. It is almost as competetive as Ivy league schools like Harvard, Yale, and Princeton. Tuition for MIT costs $55,878. Other costs, like books and access to programs are $21,142, bringing the total before aid to $77,020. With aid, the overall cost after aid is $5,084.

Why MIT?

MIT is a renowned institute with a strong academic reputation known for excellent research in engineering and computer sciences. Since my field is computer science and software development, this is an ideal place to go to so I can pursue my career. MIT is one of the top rated schools, showing how much of a prestige school it is.
